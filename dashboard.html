Escalating Plastic Bottle Waste in Indonesia

Indonesia generates millions of plastic bottles each year, and the volume continues to rise by an estimated 5% annually.
More than 60% of discarded plastic bottles are not properly managed, leading to environmental leakage through open burning, land dumping, and pollution of rivers and coastal areas. Plastic bottle collection remains heavily dependent on the informal waste sector, resulting in low efficiency and a limited recycling rate.

Source: Adapted from World Economic Forum, Radically Reducing Plastic Pollution in Indonesia: A Multi-Stakeholder Action Plan, 2020.
